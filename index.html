Sacchin Sundar

I'm a Master's student in Robotics at the University of Michigan, where I focus on neural scene representations that bridge perception and action, particularly in underwater sensing and sonar rendering. My research interests include dense correspondence learning, differentiable sonar rendering, and underwater computer vision. I'm currently working on projects involving side-scan sonar, SIREN networks, and underwater SLAM with dense correspondence using MASt3R. I serve as a Graduate Student Instructor for ROB 330 (SLAM & Navigation) and am passionate about developing robust perception systems for underwater robotics applications.

Research

I'm interested in neural scene representations, underwater computer vision, and sonar rendering. My research focuses on developing robust perception systems for underwater robotics, with particular emphasis on dense correspondence learning, differentiable sonar rendering, and neural scene reconstruction. Some projects are highlighted.

Neural Bathymetry: Differentiable Sonar Rendering for Underwater Scene Reconstruction
Sacchin Sundar, Advisor Name
Under Review, 2024
project page / arXiv

We present a novel approach to underwater scene reconstruction using differentiable sonar rendering with SIREN networks, enabling accurate bathymetry estimation from side-scan sonar data.

Underwater SLAM with Dense Correspondence using MASt3R
Sacchin Sundar, Collaborator Name
In Preparation, 2024
project page / code

Sacchin Sundar, Collaborator Name
In Preparation, 2024
project page / code
