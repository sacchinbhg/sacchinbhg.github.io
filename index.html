Sacchin Sundar

I am a Master's student in Robotics at the University of Michigan, working in the Field Robotics Group (FRoG) under the mentorship of Prof. Katie Skinner. My research centers on developing neural scene representations that serve as substrates for action, with the goal of advancing perception systems that tightly couple sensing with control and planning.

While my current projects emphasize underwater robotics-differentiable sonar rendering, neural implicit 3D reconstruction, and dense correspondence learning for underwater SLAM (e.g., MASt3R)-my broader interest lies in building physics-informed representations that generalize across robotic platforms.

I serve as a Graduate Student Instructor for ROB 330 (SLAM & Navigation) and am passionate about teaching and outreach opportunities.

BAGS: Bundle-Adjusting Gaussian Splatting for Object-Centric 3D Reconstruction
Sacchin Sundar, Ruben Fonseca
Course Project, 2025
PDF Report

We present BAGS (Bundle-Adjusting Gaussian Splatting), an extension of the Gaussian Object framework for object-centric 3D reconstruction under uncertain camera poses. Unlike standard Gaussian Splatting, which depends on accurate Structure-from-Motion outputs, our method introduces learnable pose deltas jointly optimized with Gaussian parameters through a staggered training schedule. This bundle-adjusting approach enables robust geometry recovery and pose refinement even with noisy initialization. Experiments on the MipNeRF360 dataset demonstrate that BAGS maintains high perceptual quality in novel view synthesis while recovering accurate poses, extending Gaussian Splatting to real-time robotics and low-fidelity settings where reliable camera poses are unavailable.
